So the taiko build finally moved forward this weekend. Got the oak staves from Lachance's mill out in Sainte-Julienne, $340 for the lot which honestly stung but kiln dried oak is kiln dried oak. The guy threw in two warped ones for free. "For practice" he said. Ha.

Spent saturday just planing. My shoulder was done by 2pm. The angle on the stave edges has to be dead on or the shell won't close, I redid the math three times because the first ring came out with a 4mm gap. Turns out I'd measured the drum diameter at the head instead of the belly. Dumb.

Sunday was better. Glued up half the shell, ratchet straps everywhere, kitchen smelled like Titebond. Marie was not thrilled.

Next step is the second half ring, then months of waiting while it settles before I can even think about carving the bearing edges. The hide I want is still sitting at the tannery in Granby anyway. No rush I guess. A drum takes the time it takes.
