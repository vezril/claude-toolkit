# Calvin exemplars

Real passages Calvin wrote, chosen because they *sound like him* — not because they are his most polished work. Read them to calibrate rhythm and instinct before drafting. Match the feel, don't copy the content. Wikilink brackets and vault-specific syntax have been removed; the prose is otherwise his.

Each exemplar notes what it demonstrates. When drafting for a given register, lean on the exemplars tagged for it.

---

## 1. Concept note — software (register: notes)

*Demonstrates: plain "It is a…" openers, short declarative paragraphs one idea each, functional repetition instead of synonyms, colons and examples to carry meaning, no throat-clearing.*

> It is a design practice to help design and architecture systems. It is often paired with Reactive Systems designs as they tend to go hand in hand.
>
> It will model complex systems into smaller pieces and properly defining boundaries for microservices.
>
> It is also important to maintain purity of the domains, there are techniques to do so such as Anti-Corruption Layers that can be built into microservices and legacy systems.
>
> It is important to know that from one Bounded Context to another, the meaning of a word may change. For example, in a restaurant, the word "Order" has a very specific meaning, but if you're talking to the person who manages inventory, it has another.
>
> The details of that difference is also important to remember, for example, the kitchen staff preparing an "Order" doesn't care much about the price, but to the person who pays the bill, it does.

## 2. Technical explainer (register: notes)

*Demonstrates: definition-first structure ("Also known as…"), the second-person "if you're trying to…" when a concrete scenario helps, terse trade-off statements, comfortable with mild imperfection.*

> Also known as a PSK, is a security authentication where the same password or key is being used on both the wireless access point and any connecting client devices trying to gain access to the network.
>
> There are a few problems with a PSK. Scalability is difficult. For example if you're trying to ship the PSK to 50 different people, then 50 people know that key. There is no individual user accountability because every user shares the same key.
>
> It is fully immune to the effects of EMI and does not require a shielded cables.
>
> It is however costly and complex. The tools are more expensive, and requires more specialized training to repair or wire.

## 3. Game-mechanic explanation (register: guide)

*Demonstrates: define the thing plainly, then enumerate the cases. Prose for the concept, bullets only for the genuine list. Neutral, second-person-light instructional tone.*

> Turn Meter Gain is a battle mechanic that decreases the time needed for the unit to take a turn. Abilities and effects that grant Turn Meter are normally applied to your units and falls under 3 types of application; Self, Ally, and All Allies. The amount of Turn Meter gained is different from unit to unit.
>
> - Self = Only the current unit gains Turn Meter
> - Ally = Only an Ally, chosen or at random, gains Turn Meter
> - All Allies = All units you control gain the Turn Meter
>
> Some units also have a mechanic to make themselves or other units immune to gaining Turn Meter.

## 4. Hobby / observational note (register: guide)

*Demonstrates: compact factual description, bullets for identifying features, then flowing description. Precise nouns, no filler adjectives beyond the useful ones.*

> Commonly known as the blue-gray hydnellum, it is an inedible mushroom found in North American, Europe, and some regions of Asia.
>
> Young caps have shades of blue, gray, and brown with light blue near the margin. Stem is orange to brown. The flesh is blue to black in the cap, and red to brownish in the stem. The blue hues fade with age.
>
> The cap is tomentose (has small dense hairs which gives a velvety feel). It is zonate, with concentric bands of colour sometimes apparent on the cap. When bruised, flesh stains dark inky blue.

## 5. First-person narrative (register: notes, creative)

*Demonstrates: Calvin telling a story. Longer connected sentences than his technical notes, but still plain diction. Contractions, "you do what you can", matter-of-fact about hard things.*

> I was raised to believe in The Traveler from a young age. When I was born, my mother was already a priestess. Before that, she was a criminal who would deceive others in giving her money, either through seduction or manipulation. This is how she met my father who himself is a thief, a good one as well. They are not noble professions, but when you are outcasts of a society, you do what you can to survive.
>
> Both of them stayed in their natural form as much as possible, they always said if we are to be outcast, that we should wear it as a badge of honor, that way no one can hurt you with it.

## 6. Personal goal note (register: journal)

*Demonstrates: enthusiastic first person, an exclamation where he means it, plans stated directly. This is how he writes to himself — more "I" and more energy than the notes.*

> My previous fixed objective: I shall make a 2 cho-gake shime-daiko!
>
> For shime daiko, a thickness of the edge of heads represents the size. A measure of cho-gake is used like 1 cho-gake (it is usually called namitsuke), 2 cho-gake, and 3 cho-gake. Generally, 1 cho-gake has about 11mm, 2 cho-gake has about 19mm, 3 cho-gake has about 23mm.
