# Canadian Business Registration (Quebec focus)

How to **start, register, and incorporate a business in Canada**, with a **Quebec** focus — drawn from the **Québec.ca / Registraire des entreprises du Québec (REQ)** and **CRA** business-registration pages. It lays out the structure choice and the registration steps so you can plan; it is a map, not professional advice.

> **Not legal, tax, or accounting advice.** Claude is not a lawyer, notary, or accountant. This skill summarizes published government processes so you can understand your options and ask better questions — for the actual filing, structure choice, and tax/liability consequences, consult the **REQ**, **CRA**, **Revenu Québec**, or a **lawyer/notary + accountant**. **Fees, thresholds, and rules change** — every figure here is dated (as of 2026-06; CRA pages modified 2025–26) and must be re-verified on the official sites. Cross-links: [[personal-finance]], [[canadian-registered-accounts]] (your personal RRSP/TFSA/FHSA — distinct from the business).

## Step 0 — Choose a legal form (the foundational decision)

Your form drives liability, taxes, cost, and paperwork:

- **Sole proprietorship / *entreprise individuelle* (a *travailleur autonome*)** — you *are* the business. Simplest/cheapest; **unlimited personal liability**; business income taxed on your personal return. In Quebec you generally **must register with the REQ** if you operate under a name other than your own first + last name.
- **Partnership / *société* (en nom collectif SENC, or en commandite SEC)** — two+ owners; a contract governs it; partners generally share liability (general partners unlimited). Register with the REQ.
- **Corporation / *société par actions* (inc.)** — a **separate legal person**: **limited liability**, its own tax return and (often lower) corporate tax rate, easier to raise capital and transfer ownership — but more cost, formality, and bookkeeping. You **incorporate** (constitute), then operate. Choose **federal** (Corporations Canada) or **provincial/Quebec** (REQ).
- **Cooperative** and **non-profit (OBNL/NPO)** — special forms for member-owned or mission-driven entities.

Trade-off summary: sole prop = simplest + personal liability + personal tax; corporation = liability protection + tax flexibility + more admin. The right choice depends on liability exposure, income level, reinvestment plans, partners, and growth — a question for an accountant/lawyer.

## Step 1 — Register / incorporate in Quebec (the REQ → NEQ)

- **Immatriculation (registration):** most businesses operating in Quebec must **register with the Registraire des entreprises (REQ)**, which issues the **NEQ** — the *numéro d'entreprise du Québec*, a 10-digit Quebec identifier (distinct from the federal BN). Sole proprietors under their own name may be exempt; most others must register.
- **Constitution (incorporation):** to form a *société par actions* in Quebec, you **constitute** it with the REQ (under the Quebec *Business Corporations Act*); this also gives you a NEQ. Alternatively **incorporate federally** (next step) and then register the federal corporation with the REQ to operate in Quebec.
- **Transparency obligations (since 2023):** Quebec now requires most registrants to declare their **ultimate beneficiaries** (the individuals who ultimately control/benefit) to the REQ, and to provide identity info — part of anti-fraud/anti-tax-evasion rules. Keep your REQ declaration current (annual updating declaration with your Quebec tax return).
- Keep the REQ filing **up to date** — name, address, directors, beneficiaries — or face penalties/striking off.

## Step 2 — Federal vs provincial incorporation

- **Federal (Corporations Canada, ISED):** incorporate for a fee; gives you the right to the name and to operate **across Canada**, stronger name protection. **On approval you automatically get a CRA business number (BN) and a corporation income tax (RC) program account** — no separate CRA registration for RC. You still must **register the federal corporation with the REQ** to do business in Quebec (extra-provincial registration → a NEQ).
- **Provincial/Quebec (REQ):** incorporate under Quebec law; operate primarily in Quebec. **Quebec does NOT auto-issue a BN** — you must register for the BN **separately with the CRA** (unlike AB/BC/MB/NB/NS/ON/PE/SK, which do auto-issue one).
- Federal costs a bit more and adds dual filings (federal + Quebec); provincial is simpler if you're Quebec-only. Choose by where you'll operate and brand-protection needs.

## Step 3 — The CRA business number (BN) & program accounts

The **BN** is the standard 9-digit federal identifier for your business. You need one if you **incorporate** or **register for any CRA program account**. (Unincorporated businesses only need a BN once they register for a program account.) One BN per legal entity.

A **CRA program account number** = `9-digit BN` + `2-letter program identifier` + `4-digit reference` (e.g. `123456789 RT 0001`). Common accounts:

| Account | Code | When |
|---------|------|------|
| GST/HST | **RT** | collecting GST/HST (e.g. once over the **$30,000** small-supplier threshold, or ride-share/taxi from day one) |
| Payroll deductions | **RP** | you have employees / pay salaries |
| Corporation income tax | **RC** | your business is incorporated (auto with federal/most-provincial incorporation) |
| Information returns | **RZ** | filing T5/T5018/T5013 etc. |
| Registered charity | **RR** | charity / certain non-profits |

Others (as needed): excise (RD/RE), insurance premium (RN), luxury tax (LT), underused housing (RU), air travellers security (RG), global minimum tax (PT). **Import/export (RM)** is now administered by **CBSA (CARM)**, not the CRA (since Oct 2024).

## Step 4 — The Quebec twist: Revenu Québec (TPS/TVQ)

**Critical for Quebec:** unlike the rest of Canada, **Revenu Québec administers the GST (TPS) in Quebec** as well as the **QST (TVQ)**. So in Quebec you typically **register for GST + QST through Revenu Québec** (not the CRA), and Revenu Québec also handles **source deductions** (payroll) and **Quebec corporate income tax**. Practically you may deal with **both** Revenu Québec (GST/QST, QC tax, source deductions) and the CRA (BN, federal corporate income tax, federal payroll). Register for **GST and QST** once your taxable sales exceed the **$30,000** small-supplier threshold (some activities require immediate registration). Confirm exactly what to register where on the Revenu Québec / CRA sites or with an accountant.

## Step 5 — Permits, licences & the rest

- **Business name** — choose a compliant name (Quebec has French-language and name-conflict rules via the REQ; verify availability). A registered name ≠ a trademark (federal trademark is separate via CIPO).
- **Municipal/sector permits & licences** — zoning, health, professional orders, sector-specific licences (varies by activity and municipality).
- **Bank account, insurance, bookkeeping** — open a business account (keep business/personal finances separate, esp. for a corporation), get liability insurance, set up bookkeeping/accounting from day one.
- **Obligations going forward** — annual REQ updating declaration, corporate minute book/resolutions (for inc.), tax filings (federal + Quebec), GST/QST remittances, payroll source deductions, record-keeping.
