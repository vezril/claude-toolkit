# FHSA — First Home Savings Account (CRA detail)

From the CRA FHSA pages (last modified 2026-02-02; figures **as of 2026-06**, re-verify on canada.ca). A registered plan for a **first-time home buyer** to save for a qualifying first home **tax-free**: contributions are deductible *and* qualifying withdrawals are tax-free.

## Eligibility to open

To open an FHSA you must be:
- an **individual resident of Canada**,
- at least **18** (and not more than the maximum-age rules), and
- a **first-time home buyer** — generally, you (and not at any point your spouse/common-law partner, for the FHSA test) did **not** live in a qualifying home that you (or your spouse/CLP) owned in the current year or the preceding four calendar years.

You can hold **more than one** FHSA, but the limits below apply across all of them combined.

## Participation room & limits

- **First year:** your FHSA participation room is **$8,000** in the calendar year you open your first FHSA.
- **Lifetime limit:** **$40,000** total contributions/transfers.
- **Combined:** the annual room applies to **contributions + transfers from your RRSPs**, across **all** your FHSAs. (RRSP→FHSA transfers consume room but are **not** deductible.)
- **Income earned** in the account (interest, dividends, capital gains) does **not** reduce your room — even if the account's market value exceeds your contributions.

### Carry-forward (the subtle part)
- In the year you open your first FHSA, **carry-forward = $0**.
- Each subsequent year you add the lesser-of calculations, but the **carry-forward portion is capped at $8,000**. Practically: unused room accumulates, but you can never carry more than **$8,000** of it forward, so the **maximum in any single year is $16,000** ($8,000 current + $8,000 carried).

CRA examples:
- *Vivi* opens an FHSA in 2025, contributes $6,000 → her 2026 carry-forward is $2,000 (the unused $2,000), so 2026 room = $8,000 + $2,000 = **$10,000**.
- *Wendy* opens in 2025, contributes $0 → 2026 room = **$16,000**.

## Over-contribution (excess FHSA amount)

If contributions + RRSP transfers exceed your room, the excess is taxed at **1% per month** on the highest excess amount in that month, reported on **Form RC728** (and Schedule A). A taxable withdrawal can eliminate the excess; some withdrawals restore "re-participation room." (The full re-participation-room math is intricate — see the CRA page; for most people the rule is simply *don't exceed your room*.)

## Transfers

- **RRSP → FHSA:** allowed, uses FHSA room, **not deductible**, and does **not** restore RRSP room.
- **FHSA → RRSP/RRIF:** you can transfer FHSA funds to an RRSP/RRIF **tax-free** (e.g. if you don't end up buying) — this does **not** use RRSP room and is a key escape hatch.
- Transfers must be **direct** (issuer to issuer).

## Qualifying (tax-free) withdrawal

A withdrawal is tax-free if, at the time, you meet the conditions: you're a first-time home buyer, you have a **written agreement to buy/build** a qualifying home (generally in Canada) before **Oct 1 of the year after** withdrawal, you intend to occupy it as a principal residence within one year, etc. **You may also make a Home Buyers' Plan withdrawal from your RRSP for the same home.** A **non-qualifying** withdrawal is taxable and is added to income.

## Reporting

- File **Schedule 15** with your income tax return for the year you **open** your first FHSA (even with no contributions).
- Your issuer provides a **T4FHSA** slip (box 18 contributions, box 32 RRSP transfers, box 34 spousal-RRSP transfers).
- Your **FHSA participation room** appears on your Notice of Assessment / Form T1028 / CRA account after you file.

## Maximum participation period & closing

You must close your FHSAs by **December 31** following the **earliest** of:
- the **15th anniversary** of opening your first FHSA,
- the year you turn **71**, or
- the year **after** your first qualifying withdrawal.

On closing, remaining funds can transfer **tax-free to an RRSP/RRIF** (no room used) or be **withdrawn (taxable)**.

## Practical takeaways
$8k/year, $40k lifetime, deductible in + tax-free out, carry-forward capped at $8k, combine with the HBP for the same home, and if home-buying falls through you can roll it into your RRSP tax-free. Verify all figures on the CRA page before acting.
