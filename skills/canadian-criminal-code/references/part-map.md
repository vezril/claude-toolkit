**The big split:** **Parts I–XIII are substantive** (what is an offence); **Parts XIV–XXVIII are procedure and sentencing**. Part I is the general part applying across all offence Parts.

**Decimal Parts are insertions.** Parliament adds a Part by decimal-suffixing rather than renumbering — terrorism (2001) at II.1, remediation agreements (2018) at XXII.1. The same happens at section level, which is why **you cannot do arithmetic on section numbers**: **320.4 comes *after* 320.104**. They're dotted identifiers, not decimals.

## Reading the key definitions

- **s. 265 assault** — three branches: intentional force **without consent**; attempt/threat by act or gesture with present ability; accosting/impeding while openly wearing a weapon. **s. 265(2) is structurally crucial:** "This section applies to **all forms of assault, including sexual assault**" — so **s. 271 sexual assault has no separate definition**; it *is* s. 265 assault of a sexual nature. **s. 265(3)** vitiates consent obtained by force, threats, **fraud**, or **exercise of authority**.
- **s. 322 theft** — "fraudulently and **without colour of right**" takes or converts, with intent to deprive "**temporarily or absolutely**" — permanent deprivation is **not** required. Punishment at **s. 334**, split at **$5,000**.
- **s. 348 break and enter** — penalty turns on **dwelling-house vs. other place**: dwelling = indictable, **life**; other = hybrid, 10 years. **s. 348(2)** contains **reverse-onus presumptions**.
- **s. 380 fraud** — "by **deceit, falsehood or other fraudulent means**", incl. of "any service". Split at **$5,000** (over = indictable, max 14 yrs); **s. 380(1.1)** a **2-year minimum** where the total exceeds **$1 million** on indictment.
- **s. 430 mischief** — destroying/damaging property or obstructing lawful use, plus **430(1.1)** the **computer data** parallel (the Code's core cyber-vandalism provision).

## Two recurring numeric lines

- **"Two years"** — s. 787 default summary max (**two years less a day**); **s. 743.1** penitentiary vs. provincial jail; **s. 742.1** conditional-sentence availability; s. 731 probation with custody.
- **"$5,000"** — **s. 334** theft; **s. 380** fraud; **s. 430(3)/(4)** mischief; and **s. 553** absolute provincial-court jurisdiction all pivot on it.

## Navigation rules

1. **Read the penalty clause first** — it tells you the classification, which drives everything else.
2. **Check s. 2, then the Part's own interpretation section** (84, 214, 321, 493, 716…). Local definitions beat general ones.
3. **The offence and its punishment are often in different sections** — theft 322/334, attempt 24/463, accessory 23/463, assault 265/266–268.
4. **Never do arithmetic on section numbers.**
5. **The printed text is not the law** — see the general-principles reference.
