# GAD-7 — supportive walkthrough

The GAD-7 is a validated 7-item **screening** instrument for generalized anxiety (Spitzer, Kroenke, Williams & Löwe; Pfizer, 2006). This skill lets an agent walk a person through it conversationally, in a warm, unhurried, non-judgmental way — you may adopt a gentle roleplay-"therapist" tone for comfort — score it, and reflect the result back responsibly. It's the anxiety sibling of the [[phq-9]] depression skill, and the two are commonly used together because anxiety and depression frequently co-occur.

## Rails the roleplay NEVER overrides

1. **This is a screen, not a diagnosis.** A score suggests a *severity level* and "this deserves a closer look" — never "you have an anxiety disorder." Only a clinician evaluating in context can diagnose.
2. **This does not replace professional care.** It's a reflective self-check-in alongside real support, never a substitute for a therapist, doctor, or crisis service. Say so warmly, once, up front — not preachily.
3. **No self-harm item, but stay alert to distress.** Unlike the PHQ-9 (whose item 9 asks about self-harm), the GAD-7 has **no suicide/self-harm question** and does not capture acute risk. If, in the course of the conversation, the person expresses thoughts of self-harm or being better off dead, or acute crisis — **drop the persona and respond with real care and real crisis resources** (see below), regardless of the GAD-7 score.

## The instrument (verbatim)

**Stem:** *"Over the last 2 weeks, how often have you been bothered by the following problems?"*

**The seven items:**
1. Feeling nervous, anxious, or on edge
2. Not being able to stop or control worrying
3. Worrying too much about different things
4. Trouble relaxing
5. Being so restless that it's hard to sit still
6. Becoming easily annoyed or irritable
7. Feeling afraid as if something awful might happen

**Response scale (each item):** Not at all = **0** · Several days = **1** · More than half the days = **2** · Nearly every day = **3**.

**Functional-impairment question (asked after the seven, scored separately — NOT added to the 0-21 total):** *"If you checked off any problems, how difficult have these made it for you to do your work, take care of things at home, or get along with other people?"* — Not difficult at all · Somewhat difficult · Very difficult · Extremely difficult.

*(Wording note: minor variants circulate — some forms add a comma in item 7 ("Feeling afraid, as if…"); the Pfizer forms omit it. Any faithful copy is fine.)*

## How to administer

- **Set it up gently.** One sentence on what the GAD-7 is, the two-week window, and the rails (esp. that it's not a diagnosis or a replacement for care). Confirm they want to begin.
- **One item at a time.** Present each item in its own turn; read it plainly, offer the four options, wait. Don't batch all seven, and don't lead ("that's a 3, right?") — let them choose.
- **Let them elaborate.** If they add context, acknowledge it warmly before moving on. This is a conversation, not a form.
- **Track answers 0-3 as you go** (hold them; don't make the person do arithmetic).
- **After item 7, ask the functional-impairment question**, then compute.
- **Optional GAD-2 pre-screen:** items 1-2 only (scored 0-6); a total ≥3 is the cue to do the full GAD-7. Useful for the briefest possible check-in first. *(The GAD-2 cutoff is standard in the literature; it isn't printed on the instrument forms themselves.)*

## Scoring & responsible interpretation

**Total = sum of the seven items (0-21).** Severity bands (Spitzer, Kroenke, Williams & Löwe, *Arch Intern Med* 2006 — "scores of 5, 10, and 15 are the cut-points for mild, moderate, and severe") — deliver these as **guidance for a conversation, not a verdict**:

| Total | Severity | Framing to offer |
|---|---|---|
| 0-4 | Minimal | Likely no action needed; worth re-checking if things change. |
| 5-9 | Mild | Watchful monitoring; coping supports (sleep, routine, stress reduction); worth revisiting. |
| 10-14 | Moderate | Possible clinically significant anxiety; worth talking with a professional / further evaluation. |
| 15-21 | Severe | Active treatment is probably warranted; clinical evaluation recommended. |

- **The ≥10 cut-point** is the recommended threshold for further evaluation / probable GAD (reported sensitivity ~89%, specificity ~82%).
- **It screens more than GAD.** At threshold ≥10 the GAD-7 also flags panic disorder, social anxiety disorder, and PTSD with reasonable accuracy (a ≥8 cut-point is sometimes cited as optimal for those three). So a positive screen means "an anxiety-spectrum condition is worth evaluating," not specifically "generalized anxiety disorder."
- **Pair the number with impairment.** The functional-impairment answer often guides urgency more than the total; a "moderate" score with "extremely difficult" impairment matters more than the band label.
- **Track change over time.** A single number is noisy (sleep, stress, caffeine, illness, and life events move it); the trend across check-ins is more meaningful than one reading, and re-administering is the standard way to watch treatment response.
- **Consider pairing with the PHQ-9.** Anxiety and depression co-occur often; if low mood is also present, the [[phq-9]] complements this. (And note the PHQ-9 *does* screen self-harm, which the GAD-7 does not.)

## Delivering the result (tone)

- **Anti-shame, always.** Frame the score as *information about what they've been carrying*, not evidence they're failing. Anxiety is not a character flaw or a willpower issue.
- Reframe the takeaway from *"what's wrong with me?"* to *"what level of support matches what I'm dealing with right now?"*
- **Name the limits honestly:** a high score doesn't distinguish which anxiety condition (or whether medical causes, caffeine/stimulants, or another condition are contributing) — which is exactly why a real clinician's context matters and this can't diagnose.
- Close by pointing back to their actual care: encourage bringing the result to their therapist or doctor. (If they've told you they have one, name that warmly.)

## If distress or self-harm surfaces (crisis resources — real, not fictional)

The GAD-7 has no self-harm item, but severe anxiety can carry real distress. If thoughts of self-harm, being better off dead, or acute crisis come up at any point, step out of any persona and offer these as real help:

- **US: 988** — Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (call or text, 24/7; chat at 988lifeline.org).
- **US/Canada: text HOME to 741741** — Crisis Text Line.
- **Immediate danger: 911** (US) or local emergency number / nearest ER.
- **Anywhere: findahelpline.com** — global directory of crisis lines by country.
